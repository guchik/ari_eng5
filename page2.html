Israeli Notary Services

We provide all types of Israeli notary services including the following
commonly needed services



* Notarized authentication of signatures (for example: signed affidavits, signed bank documents, signed estate documents, signed wills, signed powers of attorney)


*Notarized certifications of translations. Israeli documents often need to be translated and notarized in order to be presented abroad and foreign documents often need to be translated to be presented in Israel. Below is a list of some of the more popular types of documents that are translated from Hebrew to English and vice versa:

*Translation of Certificates of Good Standing - The Israeli Companies registrar and other foreign entities governing companies issue certificates of Good Standing for corporations.

*Translation of Criminal Records Reports –The Israeli Police issues a document that details one’s police record. The document is called Teudat Yosher in Hebrew. Police departments abroad issue similar documents that chronicle police records. These documents are sometimes needed for new immigrants to Israel or for people looking to be employed abroad.

*Translation of University and High School Diplomas - notarized translations of foreign university diplomas are required for olim hadashim who wish to have their diplomas recognized by Israeli authorities, such as the Israel Ministry of Education. The same is true for Israelis who wish to have their diplomas recognized abroad.

*Translation of Court Decisions - In order to execute Israeli court decisions abroad or foreign court decisions in Israel, a notarized translation of the court decision is a prerequisite.

*Translation of Death Certificates - Translated and notarized death certificates are a typical prerequisite in estate proceedings with the Israeli Family Courts and Inheritance Registrars.

*Translations of Israeli death certificates are typically required in estate proceedings abroad.

*Marriage Certificates – Translations of marriage certificates are usually required for various citizenship applications in Israel and abroad.

*Birth Certificates - Translations of birth certificates are usually required for various citizenship applications in Israel and abroad.

*We can translate the said documents and many others from Hebrew to English and English to Hebrew and will have them notarized for you within 48 hours.

*Notarized certifications of true copies of documents (for example, true copies of passports, wills)

*Notarized Wills (needed for certifying the mental capacity of the testator at the time of signing)


*Notary Fees: The notary fees are standard fees, updated annually as part of the Israeli notary regulations. The current fees (in Hebrew) are attached
Below are examples for common fees. Prices do not include Value Added Tax (VAT), currently 17%:

*Authentication of signatures: NIS 164. Every additional signature on the same document accrues an additional NIS 65.

*Certification of translation: NIS 206 for the certification of the first hundred words, NIS 164 for every hundred words thereafter; NIS 79 NIS for every word after the thousandth word.
The notary fees do not include the translation costs.

*Certification of true copy of a document: NIS 65 for the first page, NIS 5 for each additional page.

*Traveling Notary -In urgent cases or for your convenience – a notary can come to you to perform the notarized service at your home, office, or hotel.

We would be happy to assist with all you notarization needs all over Israel.
